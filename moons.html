Moons


Moons - also called natural satellites come in many shapes, sizes and types. They are generally solid bodies, and few have atmospheres. Most of the planetary moons probably formed from the discs of gas and dust circulating around planets in the early solar system.



There are hundreds of moons in our solar system even a few asteroids have been found to have small companion moons. The table on the right has the most recent count. Moons that begin with a letter and a year are considered provisional moons. They will be given a proper name when their discoveries are confirmed by additional observations.
